How to Get a 99+ ATAR: A Complete Study Guide

Achieving a 99+ ATAR requires more than just hard work—it needs strategic planning and efficient study techniques. Here's what worked for me and my students.

1. Start Early and Stay Consistent

Year 12 is a marathon, not a sprint. Start revising from Day 1 of Term 1. Cramming might work for a test, but ATAR exams require long-term retention.

2. Understand, Don't Memorize

Deep understanding of concepts is far more valuable than rote memorization. When you truly understand the "why" behind equations and reactions, you can apply knowledge to unfamiliar questions.

3. Practice Past Exams

  • Complete at least 5 past papers per subject
  • Time yourself under exam conditions
  • Review answers carefully—understand why you got something wrong
  • Identify patterns in questions that repeat

4. Active Recall & Spaced Repetition

Instead of re-reading notes, test yourself. Use flashcards, summarize topics from memory, and review material at increasing intervals.

5. Take Care of Your Mental Health

Burnout is real. Sleep 8+ hours, exercise regularly, and take breaks. A healthy mind performs better than an exhausted one.

SACE Mathematical Methods: Exam Strategies That Work

Mathematical Methods is one of the most challenging SACE subjects. Here's how to maximize your score:

Key Topics to Master

  • Calculus: Differentiation rules, integration techniques, applications (optimization, rates of change)
  • Functions: Graphing, transformations, domain/range
  • Statistics: Probability distributions, sampling, hypothesis testing

Exam Tips

Show all working—examiners award marks for method even if final answer is wrong. For challenging questions, break them into smaller parts you can solve.

How to Ace Your Medicine Interview: MMI Preparation Guide

The Multiple Mini Interview (MMI) is designed to assess communication skills, ethical reasoning, and suitability for medicine. Here's how to prepare:

Common MMI Stations

  • Ethical Scenarios: Discuss dilemmas like resource allocation, patient autonomy
  • Motivation: Why medicine? Why this university?
  • Current Health Issues: Stay updated on Australian healthcare topics
  • Communication: Explain complex medical concepts to a patient

Preparation Tips

Practice out loud with friends or family. Record yourself and review. Focus on structure: State your stance, provide reasoning, consider counterarguments, conclude.

SACE Chemistry: Common Student Struggles and Solutions

Top Challenges Students Face

  • Organic Chemistry: Remembering reaction mechanisms and functional groups
  • Equilibrium: Understanding Le Chatelier's principle in complex systems
  • Redox Reactions: Balancing equations and understanding electron transfer

Study Strategies

Draw mechanisms repeatedly until automatic. Create summary sheets for each topic. Teach concepts to someone else—it's the best way to identify gaps in understanding.

Year 12 Time Management: Balancing Studies and Life

With heavy coursework and exam preparation, effective time management is crucial:

  • Use a weekly planner: Block time for each subject
  • Prioritize weak areas: Spend more time on subjects where you have room to improve
  • Set realistic goals: Daily targets instead of vague "study more"
  • Eliminate distractions: Phone off during study sessions
  • Take breaks: 25 minutes study, 5 minutes break (Pomodoro technique)
